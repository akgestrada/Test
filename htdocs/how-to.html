Phone Buyer's Guide

Everything you need to know.

Availability and Pricing

Availability and Pricing can vary in different countries/regions.

Do you need a phone now?

Check if your phone is/will be available in you country.

Pricing can vary

Pricing in your country may be different. In the Philippines, the price of IPhone 14 Pro Max is ~Php 78000 while in Indonesia, the price is ~Php 83000.

Reviews!

Reviews are very important to check if there are any hidden problems.

Fake Reviews

Try to wait for trusted reviewers like MKBHD, MrWhosetheBoss, or LinusTechTips to release a FULL review. Companies stop reviewers from releasing early reviews because a bad one will hurt their sales. Sometimes, companies limit what the reviewer can talk about the device, which is abused by only allowing them to say good things about it.

Sets Expectations

There are some things you can't identify just by looking at the spec sheet. Phone cameras now are so good the only noticable thing that seperates them apart are people's personal preference (most flagship phones). Another example is the SnapDragon 8 Gen 1. Although it is a fast chip, it gets hot.

Watch reviews for each step!

To properly identify what best fits your preferences/the list of recommendations, we recommend you to watch reviews for each and every step.

Budget

How much are you willing to spend?

Be honest to yourself.

Do you actually need the best phone with the best camera and best performance? If your just going to use it for watching content or other simple things, do you really need the best phone?

IPhone Pricing

The cheapest IPhone in 2022 that we recommend is IPhone 12 Mini (Used - Php 20,000) and IPhone 12 (Used - 25,000). It is hard to recommend IPhone X because software update support will end very soon, if it hasn't already.

Android or IOS?

Which one should you choose.

It all comes down to preference.

They both have their advantages and disadvantages. If you can make use of Apple's ecosystem, go for IOS. If you like using apks to avoid paying for services, go for android.

Chipset

The minimum that we recommend is the Snapdragon 845 (30 fps, medium settings) but ideally, you should get the Snapdragon 888 (high settings, 60 fps) with 8 gigabytes of ram.

Cooling

ALWAYS check reviews first before buying because even if the smartphone has the best chipset, if the cooling is bad, you will experience throttling and the chipset won't get the performance it should. Chipsets should be cooled properly to achieve peak performance. Consider using a phone cooler/fan to get the best performance possible, but it may not be enough if internal cooling is the problem.

Candy Crush

This should not be a problem unless you are buying a very old phone (10 years ago). Even Php 5000 phones can do it.

Reviews!

There are videos online showing just how much performance a specific phone can get. To avoid fake reviews, try watching multiple videos and comparing the results.

Camera

Reviews!

Preference

What kind of photos do you like? Do you like a more colorful one? Will you use an external program to change the colors so you only need a better quality?

Reviews!

Always check reviews. 100 megapixels mean nothing if the image processing is bad. The IPhone 13 only has 12 megapixels but the quality is still one of the best!

Camera Software Updates

Some companies like Samsung have camera updates, so if you don't like the photos that it takes now, there is a chance of it being replaced, unlike Apple. If you like how your IPhone takes photos, then there shouldn't be any problems.

Audio

Check reviews to make sure.

Watching Content

Good speakers are a game changer when watching content. If you don't need it, then atleast get something decent.

Headphone Jack

We almost exclusively use wireless earphones but if you need it, check the spec sheet. If you are using high quality headphones/earphones, try watching reviews to see if the audio jack is good enough that you can actually make use of the headphones' features.

Battery Life

Check reviews to make sure.

Reviews!

The battery life shown on spec sheets are almost always not true, but there are many videos online about battery tests.

Display

What size should you get?

Display Type

What are the things that you will primary do with your phone? This determines what display type (OLED, Retina, AMOLED, etc) you should get. If you like watching content, playing games, or working(reading emails), then you would benefit from a larger screen. If you use your phone in bright environments, get a brighter screen.

Resolution

Higher resolution means better sharpness. This doesn't exactly mean better at everything because it also depends on your preference and the human eyes can only see so much from a normal distance. Higher resolution means higher battery consumption.

Refresh Rate

30 fps and 60 fps is a big jump, but 60 to 120? Not so much. If you don't play games competitively, you most likely can't see enough difference between a 120hz and 60hz refresh rate. Another thing to consider is that most videos and movies are filmed at 60fps.
