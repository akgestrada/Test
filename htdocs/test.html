Best Overall

Samsung S22 Ultra

Pros

  • Traditional Note design with modern premium touches
  • S Pen has wide-ranging functionality for work and play
  • Display quality is spectacular
  • Battery life is great
  • Ultra-grade camera system is one of the best on the market, zooming capability is hard to rival

Cons

  • S Pen has a love-it-hate-it relationship
  • Poorly communicated fast charging capability
  • Isn't called a Note and that makes us sad.

Verdict

You could not go wrong in picking the S22 Ultra. Its battery life is about as solid as you can expect from a modern-day flagship, the software is refined and feature-rich, its performance is about what you expect from a flagship, the design is understated, yet recognizable and stylish.

Best Value

Google Pixel 7

Pros

  • Compact and premium build
  • Easy to handle
  • Unique-looking design
  • Sharp, bright, color-accurate display
  • Good battery life considering its size
  • Android from the source, exclusive feature set, unrivaled perception of smoothness on this side of the OS divide
  • Superb stereo speakers
  • Overall, great camera quality with an unmatched character that has a loyal following

Cons

  • The display is just 90Hz as opposed to competitors pushing beyond 120Hz
  • Very slow charging by the standards of the day
  • Availability
  • Certain software and hardware features are regionally limited - 5G, VoLTE, and much of the onboard AI stuff
  • Ultrawide camera is underwhelming in low light
  • Selfies rarely come out tack sharp

Verdict

For around Php 30,000, the Pixel 7 is definitely one of the best value phones. It gets decent performance; one of the best phones for mobile photography, has a bright OLED screen, long battery life for its size, and exceptional software. Sadly, there are a few caveats to consider here: no true telephoto camera; display is limited to 90Hz; some advanced software features are region-dependant, and the charging solution is way too outdated. At this price, the Pixel 7 offers a unique combination of ultra-premium camera experience, long battery life and great AI-based features.

Best Foldable

Samsung Z Fold 4

Pros

  • New aspect makes cover screen more suitable for general use.
  • Samsung's foldables remain the only ones in the business with IPX8-rated waterproofing
  • Displays are roughly twice as bright as the foldable competition, on par with conventional ones
  • Impressive battery life in onscreen tests for a foldable.
  • Feature-rich software with unmatched multi-tasking functionality and S Pen support
  • Camera system is a meaningful step up

Cons

  • No change on the hinge gap situation - the gap remains. The crease remains fairly prominent, too
  • Charging speed has been improved but is still behind the competition
  • More or less the same camera system as in the S22 that runs for less than half the Fold's asking price

Verdict

We're past the point of considering foldable phones as just proof-of-concept halo devices. The gaping folded screen, a problem seen from older versions as well, has not been addressed. Cameras have been improved but could still be way better. To wrap this up, let's say that the Fold4 is not perfect. But it's not like there are readily available alternatives everywhere and the ones you could come up with have their own sets of issues. The Fold3 was already the most well-rounded and feature-packed foldable on the market then, and the Fold4 can easily claim that title now.
